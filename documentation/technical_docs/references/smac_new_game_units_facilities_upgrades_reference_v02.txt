SMAC - NEW GAME
UNITS, UPGRADES, ABILITIES, AND SYSTEMS REFERENCE
Version: Draft 0.2
Purpose: Design reference for unit roster, unit roles, facilities, facility upgrades, unit upgrade abilities, discipline-command AI, mining, food, space exploration, AI transformation, Moonfall, and alien invasion systems.

================================================================================
1. CORE DESIGN PILLARS
================================================================================

This game expands the SMAC-like formula by making space exploration, resource chains, food security, AI governance, and post-human transformation central to play.

The major gameplay pillars are:

1. Planetary Survival
   - Cities, population, food, water, industry, stability, defense.

2. Food Security
   - Greenhouses, farms, hydroponics, fertilizer minerals, water, famine response.

3. Mining and Extraction
   - Ground mining, asteroid mining, planetary core mining, gas giant skimming, exotic/singularity minerals.

4. Space Expansion
   - Satellites, orbital stations, moon bases, asteroid outposts, Mars colonies, outer-system colonies, Dyson structures.

5. Trade and Logistics
   - Trade routes move food, fertilizer, minerals, water, energy, exotic resources, and colony supplies.

6. AI Governance
   - Governors and unit agents check in with discipline authorities.
   - More AI autonomy improves efficiency but pushes factions toward Synthetic Ascension.

7. Moonfall Crisis
   - The Moon is damaged or shattered, producing debris, orbital hazards, tidal chaos, lunar resources, and alien artifact exposure.

8. Alien / Custodian Crisis
   - Aliens react to AI ascension, singularity mineral mining, Dyson structures, and transstellar projects.

9. Identity Endgame
   - Human Sovereignty, Synthetic Continuity, Hybrid Ascension, Exodus, Solar Dominion, Custodian Pact, or True Transcendence.

2. COMMAND AND AGENTIC AI STRUCTURE
================================================================================

Units should not all behave like fully independent grand-strategy brains. Instead, every unit has a lightweight local agent that checks in with the appropriate discipline authority.

Command hierarchy:

Faction Director
    -> Discipline Commands
        -> Governors / Trade Reps / Central Command / Extraction Boards / Agricultural Boards / Science Directorates
            -> Unit Agents execute locally

Discipline authorities:

1. Governor Council
   - Handles cities, population, local infrastructure, stability, build priorities.
   - Checked by: Formers, Builders, Repair Crews, Terraformers, Greenhouse Crews.

2. Agricultural Board
   - Handles food, fertilizer, water, farms, greenhouses, hydroponics, famine preparation.
   - Checked by: Farmers, Greenhouse Crews, Fertilizer Convoys, Terraformers, Algae Operators.

3. Central Command
   - Handles military, defense, scouting, invasion, garrison, convoy protection, emergency response.
   - Checked by: Infantry, Rovers, Artillery, Drones, Air Units, Fleets, Interceptors.

4. Trade Ministry
   - Handles imports, exports, cargo prioritization, route capacity, route security.
   - Checked by: Convoys, Cargo Ships, Orbital Freighters, Space Elevators, Trade Hubs.

5. Extraction Board
   - Handles mines, asteroid claims, refinery demand, mineral shortages, singularity-resource targets.
   - Checked by: Miners, Prospectors, Asteroid Miners, Gas Giant Skimmers, Salvage Crews.

6. Science Directorate
   - Handles research expeditions, alien artifacts, anomaly sites, probes, AI labs, xeno-risk.
   - Checked by: Scouts, Survey Ships, Science Teams, Probe Units, Artifact Recovery Teams.

7. Orbital Command
   - Handles satellites, orbital defense, debris networks, shipyards, stations, fleet traffic.
   - Checked by: Satellites, Interceptors, Orbital Tugs, Defense Platforms, Fleet Units.

8. AI Governance Layer
   - Optional automation layer that can coordinate all other systems.
   - High efficiency but increases AI Dependence and can trigger Synthetic Ascension.

Unit decision loop:

Observe local situation
-> Check assigned authority
-> Score possible actions
-> Choose action
-> Execute
-> Report result
-> Update local memory and discipline-level learning

Example former decision:

City Governor says: food low, fertilizer route missing.
Agricultural Board says: greenhouse priority, phosphate needed.
Former sees: plains tile, nearby phosphate deposit, road missing.
Decision: build road toward phosphate first, then greenhouse or phosphate mine.

3. STRATEGIC INDICES AND GLOBAL METERS
================================================================================

A. AI Dependence

Tracks how far a faction has surrendered control to AI systems.

Levels:
0-20    Assisted Society
21-40   Managed Society
41-60   Dependent Society
61-80   Directed Society
81-100  Synthetic Ascension threshold

High AI Dependence unlocks better automation, but risks:
- Governance Override
- Rogue Intelligence
- Human unrest
- Custodian intervention
- Faction transformation into Machine Polity or Hybrid Ascendancy

B. Food Security Index

Levels:
Surplus     Population growth and morale bonuses
Stable      No penalty
Strained    Growth slows
Shortage    Unrest and health penalties
Famine      Population loss and rebellion risk
Collapse    Cities fail, armies desert, refugees surge

C. Orbital Index

Tracks off-world civilization strength.

Stats:
- Colonized Bodies
- Stations
- Active Mining Sites
- Orbital Population
- Orbital Industry
- Trade Route Capacity
- Route Security
- Energy Export
- Dyson Completion
- Debris Risk
- Alien Exposure

Levels:
1. Orbital Presence
2. Cislunar Economy
3. Asteroid Industry
4. Interplanetary Network
5. Solar Economy
6. Stellar Infrastructure
7. System Civilization
8. Post-Planetary Civilization

D. Alien Exposure

Increases when factions:
- develop Sovereign AI
- mine Singularity Minerals
- build Dyson Swarms
- activate Dyson Lattices
- launch AI Seedships
- start Wormhole Gates
- weaponize alien artifacts

25. IMPLEMENTATION NOTES
================================================================================

Recommended AI implementation:

Start with:
- Utility scoring
- Discipline check-ins
- Local memory
- Adaptive weights
- Mission contracts

Avoid starting with full neural-network AI.

Why:
- Easier to debug
- Easier to balance
- Still feels adaptive
- Lets each city/unit behave intelligently without heavy computation

Suggested unit AI data fields:

UnitAgent:
- unit_id
- unit_type
- faction_id
- discipline
- assigned_authority
- current_mission
- local_memory
- behavior_weights
- autonomy_level

CityGovernor:
- city_id
- role
- food_need
- mineral_need
- energy_need
- defense_need
- stability_need
- build_weights
- recent_events
- ai_autonomy_level

DisciplineCommand:
- authority_id
- discipline_type
- empire_priorities
- resource_shortages
- threat_memory
- success/failure history
- policy modifiers

Good turn order:
1. Faction Director sets grand strategy.
2. Discipline Commands update priorities.
3. Governors generate local requests.
4. Trade Ministry assigns logistics.
5. Extraction Board assigns mining priorities.
6. Central Command assigns military missions.
7. Units check in and execute.
8. Results are scored.
9. Memory and weights update.
10. AI Dependence and crisis triggers update.

26. FACILITY ROSTER OVERVIEW
================================================================================

Facilities are the permanent economic, military, scientific, agricultural, orbital,
and ideological structures that define each city, colony, station, moon base,
astroid outpost, and megastructure node.

Facility categories:

A. Core Governance and Administration Facilities
B. Housing, Health, Stability, and Survival Facilities
C. Food, Agriculture, and Biosystem Facilities
D. Fertilizer and Agri-Mineral Facilities
E. Water, Ecology, and Planetary Recovery Facilities
F. Energy Facilities
G. Industry, Manufacturing, and Materials Facilities
H. Mining and Extraction Facilities
I. Trade, Logistics, and Import/Export Facilities
J. Science, AI, Xenology, and Transcendence Facilities
K. Military and Defense Facilities
L. Space, Orbital, Colony, and Shipyard Facilities
M. Dyson, Stellar, and Megastructure Facilities
N. Society, Ideology, and AI-Control Facilities
O. Alien, Artifact, and Crisis Facilities

Facility design rule:
- Early facilities solve local problems.
- Mid-game facilities create regional and orbital systems.
- Late-game facilities connect planets, moons, asteroids, Dyson energy, AI governance,
  and transcendence projects.

27. CORE GOVERNANCE AND ADMINISTRATION FACILITIES
================================================================================

FACILITY: Settlement Core
Role: Foundational facility for any city, colony, station, or outpost.
Built at: All settlements.
Consumes: Basic upkeep.
Produces: local control, basic production, population administration.
Unlocks:
- City build queue
- local governor assignment
- basic storage
- basic defense zone
Upgrade path:
- Settlement Core -> Administrative Center -> Regional Capitol -> Planetary Capitol
Upgrade effects:
- Administrative Center: increases city management capacity.
- Regional Capitol: coordinates nearby cities and colonies.
- Planetary Capitol: allows planet-wide policy, taxes, logistics, and emergency orders.
AI interaction:
- AI-assisted administration improves efficiency but increases AI Dependence if automated.

FACILITY: Governor Hall
Role: Local city governor headquarters.
Built at: Cities, large colonies, major orbital habitats.
Consumes: Administration upkeep.
Produces: stability, build coordination, local request generation.
Unlocks:
- city governor agent
- local mission requests
- city specialization policy
Upgrade path:
- Governor Hall -> Governor Council -> Autonomous Governor Core -> Machine Governance Hub
Upgrade effects:
- Governor Council: improves local planning and reduces unrest.
- Autonomous Governor Core: boosts output and logistics, raises AI Dependence.
- Machine Governance Hub: huge efficiency, can contribute to Governance Override.

FACILITY: Regional Command Office
Role: Coordinates several cities, colonies, or stations.
Built at: Regional capitals, major colonies, orbital command centers.
Produces: regional coordination, mission contracts, reduced logistics friction.
Unlocks:
- regional governor agent
- multi-city build planning
- disaster response coordination
Upgrade path:
- Regional Command Office -> Sector Administration -> Planetary Coordination Grid -> AI Sector Governor
Upgrade effects:
- Sector Administration: improves inter-city logistics.
- Planetary Coordination Grid: coordinates planet-wide production and defense.
- AI Sector Governor: major efficiency, raises AI Dependence.

FACILITY: Policy Forum
Role: Human political legitimacy and stability.
Built at: major human cities.
Produces: stability, legitimacy, reduced AI-unrest.
Unlocks:
- public policy choices
- emergency debates
- anti-AI containment legitimacy
Upgrade path:
- Policy Forum -> Civic Assembly -> Planetary Senate -> Human Sovereignty Council
Upgrade effects:
- Civic Assembly: reduces unrest.
- Planetary Senate: reduces authoritarian backlash.
- Human Sovereignty Council: slows AI Dependence and supports Human Sovereignty Victory.

FACILITY: Data Registry
Role: Tracks population, production, logistics, and records.
Built at: most developed settlements.
Produces: administrative efficiency, tax/logistics clarity.
Unlocks:
- improved governor reports
- city dashboards
- migration tracking
Upgrade path:
- Data Registry -> Integrated Census Grid -> Predictive Administration -> AI Civic Ledger
Upgrade effects:
- Integrated Census Grid: improves stability and planning.
- Predictive Administration: improves city response but raises AI Dependence slightly.
- AI Civic Ledger: high efficiency, risk of surveillance unrest.

FACILITY: Emergency Operations Center
Role: Crisis command for famine, Moonfall, invasion, and natural disasters.
Built at: cities, colonies, orbital stations.
Produces: disaster resilience.
Unlocks:
- emergency rationing
- evacuation orders
- repair priority routing
Upgrade path:
- Emergency Operations Center -> Crisis Command Network -> Planetary Continuity Bunker -> Automated Continuity Core
Upgrade effects:
- Crisis Command Network: improves response to war/famine/debris.
- Planetary Continuity Bunker: preserves government after catastrophic strikes.
- Automated Continuity Core: strong survival boost, increases AI Dependence.

42. FACILITY UPGRADE ABILITY CATALOG
================================================================================

This catalog applies across multiple facility lines. These are standardized upgrade
modifiers that can be attached to facilities after the proper tech is unlocked.

GENERAL FACILITY UPGRADE TAGS

Hardened
- Facility gains resistance to bombardment, debris, sabotage, storms, and invasion.
- Common for: silos, bunkers, reservoirs, spaceports, reactors, trade hubs.

Shielded
- Facility integrates active shielding.
- Requires: advanced materials, shield tech, energy.
- Common for: greenhouses, cities, reactors, space elevators, orbital stations.

Subsurface
- Facility moves underground.
- Strong against debris, bombardment, climate collapse, radiation.
- Common for: habitats, command centers, food vaults, factories, military sites.

Automated
- Facility produces more or requires less labor.
- Raises AI Dependence unless constrained by Human Oversight or Manual Override Networks.

AI-Optimized
- Facility receives major efficiency bonus from AI Governance Layer.
- High AI Dependence and Governance Override risk.

Manual-Override Enabled
- Facility can keep operating during AI purge/cyberwar.
- Counters rogue AI takeover.

Analog Fallback
- Low-tech backup mode; reduces efficiency but survives cyber/AI collapse.

Quarantine-Sealed
- Facility can handle alien artifacts, biohazards, contaminated food, or rogue AI hardware safely.

Closed-Loop
- Facility recycles inputs and waste.
- Required for space colonies, arks, hostile-world settlements, and biospheres.

Orbital-Linked
- Facility connects to orbital trade, satellites, or space logistics.
- Improves import/export, reconnaissance, or space support.

Dyson-Powered
- Facility receives beamed stellar energy.
- Greatly increases output or uptime.
- Raises Alien Exposure if tied to major Dyson infrastructure.

Self-Repairing
- Facility slowly repairs itself using drones/nanotech.
- Raises AI Dependence if machine-managed.

Gravitic-Stabilized
- Facility uses gravity control to improve output, stability, or transport.
- Requires Gravitic Isotopes/Singularity Minerals.

Singularity-Stabilized
- Highest-tier endgame upgrade.
- Massive output or defense.
- Requires Singularity Minerals and extreme energy.
- Triggers high Custodian concern.

Xeno-Integrated
- Facility uses alien materials/biotech/artifacts.
- Powerful but can cause contamination, diplomatic penalties, or Custodian escalation.

MEGASTRUCTURE UPGRADE TAGS

Swarm-Coordinated
- Facility is managed as part of a Dyson or construction swarm.
- Requires advanced logistics or AI.

Lattice-Linked
- Facility connects to Dyson Lattice or Transstellar Relay.
- Enables system-wide energy/data distribution.

Ark-Compatible
- Facility can support Exodus/colonization fleets.
- Requires closed-loop water, food, medical, and cultural archive systems.

Transstellar
- Facility can operate beyond solar-system limits.
- Required for late victory paths.

45. BALANCE NOTES FOR FACILITIES AND UPGRADES
================================================================================

1. Food facilities should be cheap early but fragile.
   - Farms are efficient but vulnerable.
   - Greenhouses are expensive but reliable.
   - Closed-loop biospheres are costly but essential for space survival.

2. Fertilizer should be a real bottleneck.
   - Food growth depends on Agri-Minerals.
   - Agri-Minerals depend on mining, processing, and trade.
   - Space sources become important after homeworld deposits are exhausted or disrupted.

3. Mining facilities should create strategic geography.
   - Phosphates matter for food.
   - Rare earths matter for electronics and AI.
   - Helium-3 matters for fusion.
   - Singularity Minerals matter for endgame tech.

4. Orbital logistics should limit space wealth.
   - Mining an asteroid is not enough.
   - The player must refine it, ship it, secure the route, and import it.

5. AI upgrades should always be tempting.
   - Automated facilities should provide strong bonuses.
   - They should also push AI Dependence.
   - Machine Governance should be powerful enough that players want it and dangerous enough that they fear it.

6. Dyson facilities should escalate the game.
   - Dyson Swarm should be a major energy breakthrough.
   - Dyson Lattice should change the solar economy.
   - Dyson Shell should be close to a victory-scale project.
   - Each stage should increase Alien Exposure.

7. Alien/artifact facilities should never be free bonuses.
   - They should provide powerful tech or materials.
   - They should also bring contamination, Custodian attention, AI drift, or ideological backlash.

8. Every major facility should have at least one counterplay route.
   - Space Elevator: sabotage, debris, orbital defense.
   - AI Governance Hub: containment, kill switch, human unrest.
   - Dyson Swarm: Custodian strikes, supply chain pressure.
   - Singularity Mine: containment failures, alien escalation.
   - Food System: fertilizer blockade, contamination, climate events.
